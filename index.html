Game Overview

Enter the Arena

Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave is the eighteenth mainline entry in Nintendo's celebrated tactical RPG series, developed by Intelligent Systems exclusively for the Nintendo Switch 2. Announced as the dramatic closing reveal of the September 12, 2025 Nintendo Direct, the game immediately ignited fan excitement worldwide.

"The Heroic Games have begun. A brand new entry in the Fire Emblem series is set to enter the arena."
— Nintendo

The story unfolds within a grand gladiatorial event known as the Heroic Games, hosted by the mysterious Divine Sovereign — a figure who promises to grant any wish to the victor. Warriors from all walks of life converge on the massive arena city of Dagsion, each driven by deeply personal motivations: honor, survival, redemption, or power.

Set in what appears to be the same universe as Fire Emblem: Three Houses, Fortune's Weave features familiar elements — Heroes' Relics, Crests, Demonic Beasts — but in a strikingly different era inspired by ancient Roman and Greek culture, with colosseums, chariots, and toga-draped nobles. The reveal trailer's final moments stunned fans with the appearance of an older Sothis, confirming a link to the beloved Fódlan continuity.

Four protagonists — Cai, Theodora, Dietrich, and Leda — are spotlighted in the debut trailer, each entering the Heroic Games for their own reasons. Fans and analysts speculate the game may feature branching storylines similar to Three Houses, with multiple character paths weaving together.

Characters

Warriors of the Arena

Red Faction
Cai
Flying-Ornius Rider · Lance Fighter
"I want to save my father — that's all there is to it."
A young man from a rural village who enters the Heroic Games to save his gravely ill father. Skilled with the lance, he bears the Crest of Aubin and rides an ostrich-like bird known as an Ornius into battle.
Blue Faction
Theodora
Noble · Queen of Salamis
"I need the strength to lead my people — strength enough for a queen!"
A powerful queen from the nation of Salamis who commands a formidable army. She wields Mahadu's Spear and leads battalion-backed Gambits that can immobilize entire formations of enemies in a single strike.
Yellow Faction
Dietrich
Swordsman · Relic Bearer
"I need to face it again."
A mysterious white-haired swordsman bearing the Crest of Lamine and wielding the Answerer — a Hero's Relic whose blade can unfold sharp prongs on command. His Moving Shadow Blaze Art lets him teleport across the battlefield.
Dark Faction
Leda
Dancer · Beast Summoner
"There's a man I hate so much that simply killing him will not satisfy me."
A captivating dancer who enters the Heroic Games driven by a burning desire for revenge. Her Song of Beasts Blaze Art calls forth towering Demonic Beasts to fight at her side — a power that echoes the darkest forces of Fódlan.

✦ The Divine Sovereign — the emerald-haired, pointy-eared host of the Heroic Games — may be a Nabatean or dragonkind figure. An adult Sothis also appears at the trailer's climax, bearing the iconic green hair and crown.

Gameplay

Strategy Reforged

Fortune's Weave returns to the tactical grid-based combat that defines the Fire Emblem series, building on the foundation of Three Houses while introducing exciting new elements. The massive colosseum city of Dagsion appears to serve as a social hub — comparable to the Monastery in Three Houses — where players can explore freely between battles.

⚡
Blaze Arts (New)
Brand-new mechanic allowing units to spend HP to unleash powerful special abilities directly on the battlefield — echoing the blood magic of Fire Emblem Gaiden.
🎯
Combat Arts Return
Weapon-based combat arts return from Three Houses, giving each unit a repertoire of specialized attacks tied to their equipped weapon.
🛡️
Gambits & Battalions
Battalions and their gambit attacks make a comeback, letting commanders call upon armies of soldiers to support their fighters in tactical ways.
💎
Crests & Relics
The Crest system from Three Houses returns alongside the legendary Hero's Relics — mystical weapons bound to bloodlines, including new additions like the Answerer.
🐉
Demonic Beasts
The monstrous Demonic Beasts of Fódlan return as battlefield threats and even summonable allies, with designs reminiscent of their Three Houses counterparts.
🗡️
Weapon Durability
Weapon durability returns after being absent in Engage — adding a strategic resource management layer. A new "Curse" stat also appears in combat forecasts.
🐎
Dismounting
Mounted units can dismount during battle, a returning mechanic that provides tactical flexibility when terrain or unit positioning calls for it.
🗺️
Free Roam Hub
The arena outskirts appear to feature free exploration — a social hub where players interact with characters, manage their party, and prepare for upcoming battles.
